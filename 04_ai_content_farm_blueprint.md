## Part 3: Account architecture

### The identity framework

Each account needs:
- **Name:** Memorable, niche-relevant, available across platforms
- **Bio:** Clear value proposition, who you help, CTA
- **Visual identity:** Consistent colors, fonts, profile pic style
- **Voice:** Distinct personality (educational, witty, motivational, blunt)

### Account setup template

```
Account name: [name]
Primary platform: [where you post first]
Secondary platform: [cross-post here]
Niche: [specific focus]
Target audience: [demographics + psychographics]
Voice: [personality description in 1 sentence]
Content mix: [% educational / % entertaining / % promotional]
Primary revenue: [main money source]
Secondary revenue: [backup money source]
Posting frequency: [posts per day]
```

### Cross-promotion rules

- Never cross-promote between accounts in the SAME niche
- Cross-promote between accounts in DIFFERENT niches when there's natural overlap
- Example: Your fitness account shares your wellness account. Your finance account never mentions your meme account.
- Keep a "portfolio map" showing which accounts link to which

## Part 4: The AI content generation system

### The 3-tier content approach

**Tier 1: AI-generated base (70% of content)**
Bulk-generate content with ChatGPT/Claude. Social posts, short scripts, captions.

**Tier 2: AI-assisted human touch (20% of content)**
Start with AI draft, add personal opinion, specific example, or hot take.

**Tier 3: Original/reactive (10% of content)**
React to trending topics. Share personal experiences. Engage with current events.

### Bulk generation workflow

**Step 1: Weekly content batch (Monday, 60 minutes for all 5 accounts)**

For each account, generate 7-14 posts:

```
You are [ACCOUNT PERSONALITY].
Your audience: [TARGET AUDIENCE].
Your niche: [SPECIFIC NICHE].
Your voice: [VOICE DESCRIPTION].

Generate [NUMBER] [PLATFORM] posts for this week.

Content types needed:
- [NUMBER] educational/value posts
- [NUMBER] engagement posts (questions, polls, hot takes)
- [NUMBER] story/personal posts
- [NUMBER] promotional posts (linking to product/affiliate)

Rules:
- Every post must have a hook in the first line
- Include specific numbers or examples in value posts
- No AI vocabulary (leverage, delve, comprehensive, etc.)
- Platform-specific: [Twitter: under 280 chars / Instagram: 150 words + 30 hashtags / TikTok: script for 30-60 second video]
```

**Step 2: Quality review (30 minutes)**

Scan all generated content for:
- Anything that sounds robotic (rewrite)
- Factual claims that need verification (check or remove)
- Posts too similar to each other (vary)
- Platform-specific formatting issues

**Step 3: Schedule (30 minutes for all accounts)**

Load into Buffer/Publer via CSV or manual queue.

### Platform-specific content adapters

**Twitter/X adapter:**
```
Adapt this content for Twitter. Rules:
- Under 280 characters
- Hook in first line
- No hashtags (Twitter de-prioritizes them)
- No external links in the tweet body (put in reply)
- Conversational, direct tone
```

**Instagram adapter:**
```
Adapt this content for Instagram. Rules:
- Caption: 150-300 words
- Hook in first line (only 2 lines visible before "...more")
- End with question or CTA
- Add 20-30 relevant hashtags (mix of big and small)
- Suggest an image concept for the post
```

**TikTok adapter:**
```
Adapt this content for TikTok video. Rules:
- Script length: 30-60 seconds spoken
- Hook in first 3 seconds (question or bold claim)
- One clear point per video
- End with: follow for more [topic]
- Suggest trending sound category
```

**YouTube Shorts adapter:**
```
Adapt this content for YouTube Shorts. Rules:
- Script: 30-50 seconds
- Hook in first 2 seconds
- Visual-first (describe what the viewer sees)
- No CTA to subscribe (YouTube does this automatically for Shorts)
- Suggest text overlay for key points
```

## Part 5: Visual content generation

### Image content (Instagram, Pinterest, Twitter)

**Quote graphics:**
Use Canva templates. Create 5 templates per account (different layouts, same brand colors). Swap the quote text for each post. 2 minutes per image.

**Carousel posts:**
```
Create a 7-slide Instagram carousel about [TOPIC]:

Slide 1: Bold hook headline on branded background
Slides 2-6: One tip per slide, large text, minimal design
Slide 7: Summary + CTA (follow for more, save this post)

Colors: [YOUR BRAND COLORS]
Font: [YOUR BRAND FONT]
```

Generate the text in ChatGPT/Claude, design in Canva.

**AI-generated images:**
For unique visuals, use Midjourney or Leonardo.ai:
- Fitness niche: workout illustrations, healthy food photos
- Faith niche: nature scenes with scripture overlay
- Finance niche: graph/chart visualizations
- Motivation niche: minimalist quote backgrounds

### Video content (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)

**Faceless video formula:**
1. AI generates script (30-60 seconds)
2. Record screen, stock footage, or AI-generated visuals
3. AI voice narration (ElevenLabs) or text-to-speech
4. Auto-captions in CapCut
5. Add trending/relevant background music (quiet)
6. Export vertical (9:16)

**Production time:** 15-20 minutes per video once you have a template.

**Volume target:** 3-5 videos per account per week. With 5 accounts: 15-25 videos/week. At 20 minutes each: 5-8 hours/week of video production.

## Part 6: Growth tactics per platform

### Twitter/X growth

- Post 2-3 times per day
- Reply to 10 large accounts in your niche daily
- Thread format for best reach (1 thread per week)
- Never use external links in the main tweet

### Instagram growth

- Post 1 Reel + 1 carousel per day
- Use 20-30 hashtags (mix of 100K-1M and 10K-100K)
- Post to Stories daily (behind the scenes, polls, Q&A)
- Engage with 20 accounts in your niche daily (comments, not just likes)

### TikTok growth

- Post 2-3 videos per day (algorithm rewards volume)
- First 3 seconds determine everything (hook immediately)
- Use trending sounds when possible
- Respond to comments with video replies (the algorithm loves this)

### YouTube growth (long-form and Shorts)

- Shorts: 3-5 per week (drive subscribers)
- Long-form: 1-2 per week (drive watch time and revenue)
- Thumbnails and titles matter more than content quality
- First 30 seconds must hook or they leave

### Pinterest growth

- Pin 10-15 times per day (use Tailwind for scheduling)
- Rich Pins for product links
- Long-form pins (1000px+ height) perform best
- Board descriptions with keywords for SEO

## Part 9: Account security and risk management

### Don't put all eggs in one platform

If 80% of your revenue comes from TikTok and TikTok bans your account, you lose 80% of income. Distribute across platforms.

**Rule of thirds:**
- No single platform should be more than 40% of revenue
- No single account should be more than 30% of revenue
- Always have an owned audience (email list) as backup

### Anti-ban practices

- Don't use automation tools that violate TOS
- Don't buy followers or fake engagement
- Don't post identical content across platforms (adapt for each)
- Don't link to sketchy products or scams
- Have a backup email for each account (not all on one email)

### The email list safety net

Every account should drive to a newsletter/email list:
- Platform goes down: you still have email
- Account gets banned: email your list where to find you
- Algorithm changes: email reaches 30-50% of subscribers vs. 5-10% on social

Build the list from day 1 on every account.
