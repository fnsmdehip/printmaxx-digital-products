**Price: $17**

---

## What this is

A complete tool-by-tool breakdown organized by how much money you're making. Because the tools that make sense at $0/month are different from what makes sense at $10K/month.

No affiliate links influencing recommendations. No "best tool ever" hype. Just what works, what it costs, what it replaces, and when to upgrade.

Every tool in this guide has been evaluated on: cost, time saved, revenue impact, and whether a free alternative exists. If there's a free option that's 80% as good, I'll tell you.

---

## How this guide works

There are 4 revenue tiers. Start at your current tier. Only upgrade when you hit the next tier AND a tool is actually bottlenecking you.

**Rule: Never spend more than 60% of revenue on tools and team.** If you're making $500/month, your total tool spend should be under $300/month. The rest is profit (or reinvestment into paid ads and growth).

| Tier | Revenue | Total Tool Budget | Focus |
|------|---------|------------------|-------|
| 0 | $0-500/mo | $0-150/mo | Launch everything cheap |
| 1 | $500-2K/mo | $150-400/mo | Upgrade bottlenecks |
| 2 | $2K-10K/mo | $400-1,500/mo | Scale output + paid acquisition |
| 3 | $10K+/mo | $1,500-5,000/mo | Production-grade + team |

## Category 1: AI writing and content

**What you need:** Draft blog posts, social content, emails, product descriptions, ad copy, and scripts.

### Tier 0: $0/month

**ChatGPT (free tier)**
- 3.5-level responses, decent for drafts
- Limited to shorter conversations
- Good for: social posts, short emails, product descriptions

**Claude.ai (free tier)**
- Better at longer content, follows instructions more precisely
- Limited daily messages
- Good for: blog posts, detailed outlines, content strategies

**Google Gemini (free)**
- Multimodal (text + images)
- Good for brainstorming and research
- Handles large context windows well

**Use this combo:** Claude for long-form drafts, ChatGPT for quick social posts, Gemini for research with lots of source material.

### Tier 1: $20/month

**ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) OR Claude Pro ($20/month)**
Pick one. Not both.

ChatGPT Plus if: You need image generation (DALL-E built in), you use lots of plugins, or you prefer a conversational style.

Claude Pro if: You write long-form content, you need precise instruction-following, or you work with large documents.

**When to upgrade:** When free tier limits are costing you more than 30 minutes/day in workarounds.

### Tier 2: $20-120/month

**Claude Pro ($20/month) + ChatGPT Plus ($20/month)**
Now you can afford both. Use each for its strengths.

**OR Claude Max ($100/month)** if you're using Claude Code for app building (unlimited Opus).

**Add: Jasper ($49/month)** -- Only if you're producing 50+ pieces of content per week and need brand voice consistency. Otherwise unnecessary.

### Tier 3: $100-200/month

**Claude Max ($100/month)** for heavy AI usage + coding
**ChatGPT Plus ($20/month)** as backup and for DALL-E
**API access** for automation workflows (pay per use, usually $10-50/month)

## Category 2: AI image generation

**What you need:** Social media graphics, blog images, product mockups, thumbnails, persona images.

### Tier 0: $0/month

**Canva (free tier)**
- Templates for everything: social posts, presentations, thumbnails
- Limited but functional AI image generation
- Good enough for 90% of solopreneur visual needs

**Ideogram (free tier)**
- Best free AI image generator for text-in-images
- 25 free generations/day
- Better than DALL-E for logo-style graphics

**Leonardo.ai (free tier)**
- 150 tokens/day (roughly 30 images)
- Good quality for social media assets
- Consistent style generation

### Tier 1: $12-13/month

**Leonardo.ai Apprentice ($12/month)**
- 8,500 tokens/month
- Better quality models
- Good for: bulk social graphics, persona images, product visuals

**OR Canva Pro ($13/month)**
- Background remover, brand kit, premium templates
- AI image generation built in
- Better for: non-technical users who want drag-and-drop

**Pick based on your main need:** Mostly photo-realistic or artistic images = Leonardo. Mostly templated graphics with text = Canva Pro.

### Tier 2: $29-42/month

**Midjourney ($10/month basic, $30/month standard)**
- Highest quality AI images
- Best for: hero images, thumbnails, marketing visuals
- Requires Discord (slight learning curve)

**+ Canva Pro ($13/month)** for post-processing and templates

### Tier 3: $50-100/month

**Midjourney Standard ($30/month)** for premium visuals
**Leonardo Premium ($30/month)** for bulk generation
**Canva Pro ($13/month)** for assembly and templates
**Flux/Stable Diffusion (self-hosted, $0)** for unlimited generation if you have a capable GPU

## Category 9: Payments and monetization

**What you need:** Accept payments, sell digital products, manage subscriptions.

### Tier 0: $0/month (pay per transaction)

**Stripe** -- 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
- Industry standard
- Handles subscriptions, one-time, and marketplace payments
- Free to set up

**Whop** -- For digital products
- Built-in community, courses, and product hosting
- Transaction fee only
- Better discovery than Gumroad

**Gumroad** -- 10% flat fee per transaction
- Simple setup, good for first digital product
- Higher fee than Stripe direct but easier to use
- Note: Whop takes less per transaction at scale

### Tier 1: Same tools, no change needed

Payment processors scale with you. No reason to upgrade until you're doing $10K+/month.

### Tier 2: Same + add

**LemonSqueezy** as Gumroad alternative -- Lower fees, better checkout
**RevenueCat** for in-app subscriptions if you have apps

### Tier 3: Add specialized tools

**Paddle** for international tax handling
**Chargebee** for complex subscription management
**Stripe Atlas** for incorporating as a US company (if international)

## When to upgrade (decision framework)

Don't upgrade because a tool is "better." Upgrade because the current tool is costing you time or revenue.

**Upgrade when:**
- You hit the free tier limit more than 3 times per week
- A task takes 2x longer because of tool limitations
- You're losing money by not having a feature (e.g., no email warmup = spam folder)
- The upgrade pays for itself within 30 days (calculate: time saved x your hourly rate)

**Don't upgrade when:**
- You're not using the current tool to its full potential
- You're upgrading because someone recommended it on Twitter
- The free tier handles your current volume
- You're spending money before making money

### The upgrade test

Before buying any tool, answer these 3 questions:
1. What specific problem does this solve that my current setup doesn't?
2. How many hours per week will this save me?
3. Will this directly lead to more revenue within 30 days?

If you can't answer all 3 specifically, you don't need the upgrade yet.

## Your action plan

1. **Identify your tier** based on current monthly revenue
2. **Audit your current tools** -- are you paying for things you don't use?
3. **Fill gaps** from the recommended stack for your tier
4. **Set a calendar reminder** to re-evaluate tools every quarter
5. **Rule of thumb:** If a free tool does 80% of what the paid one does, use the free one until you need the other 20%

The best tool stack is the one you actually use. Don't optimize your stack. Optimize your output.
